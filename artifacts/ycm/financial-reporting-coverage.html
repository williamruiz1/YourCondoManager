Why Cash Flow was the pick. It was the one standard HOA report genuinely absent from the reports surface — and the only missing one that's accurately buildable from the live data path. Balance Sheet and the Operating-vs-Reserve fund breakdown both exist server-side but depend on the General Ledger, which ships default-OFF; building a second, non-GL version would be inaccurate. Budget-vs-Actual already exists on the Budgets page. So Cash Flow was the highest-value, cleanly-buildable gap.

What the Cash Flow report shows

  • Cash In — owner-ledger payments received, dated by post date.
  • Cash Out — vendor invoices (by invoice date, excluding drafts/voids) + utility payments (by paid/due date), grouped by expense category.
  • Net Cash Flow, a monthly cash-in / cash-out / net table, and a cash-out-by-category breakdown.
  • Cash-basis approximation; the cash-out figures reconcile with the Budget-vs-Actual actuals (same source data).
  • Read-only — moves no money, writes no ledger or GL table.
