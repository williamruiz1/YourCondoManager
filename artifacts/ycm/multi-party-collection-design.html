YCM — Multi-Party Money Collection on Stripe Connect

How owners' dues, HOA money, and property-manager fees move — and who is allowed to hold what. This document is a proposal for review. The code that ships with it is behind a feature flag that is OFF by default; the live money path is unchanged. Generated 2026-06-24.

Owner (pays dues) HOA / condo association Property Manager (PM) Your Condo Manager (platform)

The one rule that governs everything

YCM is a software platform, never a bank. Money always settles to the account of the party it belongs to — the HOA's money goes to the HOA's own Stripe account, the PM's fee goes to the PM's own Stripe account. YCM only ever pulls its own small platform fee, and the funds never sit in or pass through a YCM-controlled balance. This keeps YCM out of "money transmitter" territory (where you'd need money-transmission licenses and trust-fund handling that a SaaS shouldn't take on).

Mechanically: every charge in all three flows is a Stripe "direct charge" on the rightful party's connected account (platform secret key + Stripe-Account: acct_… header). YCM's cut rides as an application_fee_amount; a second party's cut (when there is one) rides as a separate Transfer straight to that party's account. At no point does Stripe move the principal into the YCM platform balance.

Flow 1 — Self-managed HOA Live — reference pattern, do not change

Owner →pays dues HOA connected account ⤿1% app fee YCM platform balance

Plain English: an owner pays their dues; the full amount lands in their HOA's own Stripe account and pays out to the HOA's bank. YCM skims a 1% platform fee (with a $0.50 floor and $25 cap) at the same moment.

Stripe mechanism: Direct charge + application fee

  • Charge created with the platform secret key + header Stripe-Account: <hoa acct_…> → the charge lives on the HOA's account ("direct charge").
  • application_fee_amount moves YCM's cut to the platform balance automatically.
  • Funds settle to the HOA; YCM never holds them.

Where the account id lives: payment_gateway_connections.providerAccountId (the HOA's acct_…), Connect state in metadataJson._connect. Resolved by resolveConnectChargeRouting(associationId).

Code today: server/services/stripe-connect-resolver.ts → callers payment-portal.ts, autopay.ts, retry-service.ts pass stripeAccountHeader + applicationFeeCents into payment-service.ts. Untouched by this work.

Flow 2 — PM management fee New Flag off by default

HOA (payer) →pays mgmt fee PM connected account ⤿platform fee YCM platform balance

Plain English: a property manager bills the HOAs it manages for its management fee. The PM is the merchant of record — the money is the PM's revenue and settles to the PM's own Stripe account. YCM takes a platform fee.

Stripe mechanism: Direct charge + application fee (same shape as Flow 1, PM-as-destination)

  • Charge with platform key + Stripe-Account: <pm acct_…> → lands on the PM's account.
  • application_fee_amount routes YCM's platform fee to YCM.
  • The HOA is the payer (its saved bank/card), the PM is the recipient. No third party splits this charge — it's a single-recipient charge, so the simplest correct mechanism is identical to Flow 1.

Why not a split here: there are only two parties (payer HOA, recipient PM) plus YCM's fee. A two-party-plus-platform-fee charge is exactly the Flow 1 pattern; introducing transfers would add risk for no benefit.

Where the PM account id lives: a new connected-account relationship is required — the PM has its own acct_…, distinct from any HOA. See "Storage" below.

Flow 3 — PM collects owner dues for a managed HOA New Flag off by default

Owner →pays dues HOA connected account ⤿PM's cut PM connected account
and HOA connected account ⤿platform fee YCM platform balance

Plain English: owners pay dues through the PM relationship, but the dues belong to the HOA. The money must land in the HOA's own account (or a designated segregated/trust account — see decision below), the PM gets its agreed cut, and YCM gets its platform fee. This is a three-way split.

Stripe mechanism: Direct charge on the HOA account + separate Transfer of the PM fee + application fee

  • Charge with platform key + Stripe-Account: <hoa acct_…> → the principal lands on the HOA's account (the dues belong to the HOA).
  • application_fee_amount → YCM's platform fee to YCM.
  • A separate Transfer from the HOA's balance to the PM's connected account (destination = <pm acct_…>) moves only the PM's agreed cut. The HOA keeps the remainder.

Critical: the PM's cut is a transfer out of the HOA's balance to the PM — it never lands in, or passes through, a YCM-controlled balance. YCM stays a router, not a holder.

Why this mechanism and not the alternative

MechanismVerdictWhy
(chosen) Destination charge on HOA + Transfer of PM cutUsePrincipal settles to the rightful owner (HOA) directly; PM cut is a clean transfer to the PM; YCM only ever sees its fee. No YCM-held funds.
Separate charge on the YCM platform + transfers to HOA & PMRejectRoutes the HOA's dues through the YCM platform balance first. That makes YCM a holder/transmitter of other people's money — exactly what we must avoid.
Destination charge with on_behalf_of set to PMReject (for the default)Makes the PM the merchant of record for the HOA's money — muddies whose money it is and complicates trust-account segregation. Revisit only if a state requires PM-as-MoR.

⚠ Decision required — William / legal counsel sign-off (NOT decided here)

Flow 3 moves money that belongs to the HOA but is collected through the PM relationship. Many states' real-estate / property-management law require that funds belonging to a client (the HOA) be segregated from the PM's operating funds — i.e. held in a designated trust / escrow account, not commingled with the PM's own money. This is a legal/compliance decision, not an engineering one. The code is built so either option can be wired in without a rewrite; no compliance assumption is hardcoded.

Fund-routing options for Flow 3

Option A — Dues settle directly to the HOA's own connected account; PM fee is a separate transfer to the PM.
  • Pro: the HOA's money is in the HOA's own account from the first second — arguably the cleanest segregation (it never touches the PM at all). YCM never holds it.
  • Consideration: requires each managed HOA to have its own Connect account. Confirm with counsel that "HOA's own Stripe account, PM never custodies it" satisfies the applicable trust-account rule (it usually does, because the PM never holds the funds).
Option B — Dues settle into a designated trust / segregated sub-account (held for the HOA), distinct from the PM's operating funds; PM fee swept to the PM.
  • Pro: matches the literal "broker trust account" model some states prescribe, where the PM administers a segregated client-trust account.
  • Consideration: requires modeling a trust/escrow connected account and the sweep mechanics; more moving parts; the segregated account must still never be a YCM-controlled balance.

The ask: William + counsel choose A or B (or a per-state policy) and confirm the trust-account handling. Until then, Flow 3's routing target is a configurable policy defaulting to Option A (HOA-direct), and the flag stays off.

Reversibility & safety

  • Feature flag MULTI_PARTY_CONNECT_ENABLED — default OFF. Mirrors GL_ENABLED. When off, flows 2 & 3 resolvers return null and every existing caller falls through to the unchanged Flow 1 / manual-key path.
  • Flow 1 is byte-behavior-identical when the flag is off. The existing resolveConnectChargeRouting is not modified; new logic lives in new functions/files gated by the flag.
  • No live money movement is wired on by default. The build adds routing computation + storage; it does not point any live charge path at the new flows until the flag is on AND the trust-account decision is made.
  • No live Stripe mutating calls in tests. All new routing is unit-tested against mocks.
