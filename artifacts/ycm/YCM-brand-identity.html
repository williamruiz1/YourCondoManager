Logo System

The ascending-bars mark

Four bars rising left → right — upward community performance and operational momentum. The tallest, rightmost bar carries the teal accent. Default to the horizontal lockup; drop to mark-only only when the wordmark is nearby or space is tight. Minimum clear space on all sides = the width of one bar.

Horizontal lockupDefault · web, letterhead, email, signage
Stacked lockupSquare / tall spaces · social, splash, posters

Color

Palette

A two-tone teal system. Deep teal is the brand ink; teal accent is the interactive layer. Light teal, gray, and off-white carry surfaces and structure.

Deep Teal#014D4A

Primary. Wordmark, headings, primary buttons, nav, dark surfaces.

Teal Accent#15A39C

Links, active/focus, progress, the tallest logo bar, highlights.

Light Teal#BFE8E4

Tints, hover/selected, soft badges, reversed mark, chart fills.

Gray#E5E7EB

Borders, dividers, disabled, table rules, card outlines.

Off-White#F5F7F9

Page background, app canvas, section fills behind white cards.

Usage

Do's & Don'ts

Guardrails that keep the brand consistent across every surface.

Do

  • Keep one bar-width of clear space around every lockup.
  • Use the horizontal lockup as the default.
  • Reverse to white/light-teal mark on deep-teal backgrounds.
  • Keep the tallest bar as the teal accent.
  • Set all wordmarks in Inter Tight Bold.

Don't

  • Recolor the mark outside the palette (no blue, no #2DBDB0).
  • Add gradients, shadows, bevels, or 3D.
  • Stretch, skew, rotate, or rearrange the bars.
  • Use accent teal for small body text on white (contrast fail).
  • Drop ".org" from the wordmark in formal lockups.
