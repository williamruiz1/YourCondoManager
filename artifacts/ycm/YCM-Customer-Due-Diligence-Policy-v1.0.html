1. Purpose & Scope

This policy governs how YourCondoManager (“YCM”) identifies, verifies, and onboards the business customers and end users that connect financial accounts through YCM’s platform. YCM operates a multi-tenant SaaS platform for residential community association financial management. Plaid is used on a read-only basis (Transactions product only) to import account activity for reconciliation; no funds are moved through Plaid.

YCM serves two categories of business customer, each of which is provisioned as an isolated sub-account and is subject to the due diligence below:

  • Property management companies — firms that administer one or more associations on the YCM platform.
  • Community associations — condominium and homeowner associations (HOAs), whether self-managed or managed by a property management company.

2. Customer Due Diligence — Property Management Companies

Before a property management company is activated on the platform, YCM performs and records:

  • Business-entity verification: legal business name, entity type, formation/registration jurisdiction, and principal business address.
  • Authorizing person (control person) verification: identity, title, and authority of the principal or officer who signs up and binds the company.
  • Beneficial-ownership review: identification of beneficial owners where the entity type warrants it.
  • Sanctions / watchlist screening: screening of the company and its authorizing person against U.S. sanctions and watchlists (OFAC).
  • Prohibited-use / risk classification: confirmation that the company operates exclusively in low-risk residential community management and in none of the prohibited verticals in Section 5.

3. Customer Due Diligence — Community Associations (HOAs)

Before an association is activated and any association bank account is connected, YCM performs and records:

  • Association verification: confirmation that the association is a bona fide community association — legal/association name, evidence of existence (governing documents or state registration), property address, and unit count.
  • Authorizing officer verification: identity, board role (e.g., treasurer/president) or property-manager authority, and authority to act for the association in connecting its account.
  • Sanctions / watchlist screening: screening of the association and its authorizing officer against U.S. sanctions and watchlists (OFAC).
  • Prohibited-use / risk classification: confirmation the association engages only in residential community management.

Note: community associations are typically non-stock, non-profit corporations governed by an elected board and without conventional beneficial owners; diligence therefore focuses on verifying the association’s legitimacy and the authority of the connecting board officer or manager.

4. Authorization & Consent (account connection)

  • Before any Plaid connection, the user receives the required disclosures and consents to data access; consent is recorded (user, policy version, timestamp, IP).
  • The user authenticates directly with the financial institution through Plaid Link; YCM never sees or stores bank login credentials.
  • YCM stores only an encrypted Plaid access token, the Plaid item identifier, institution/account names, the account-number mask (last four only), type/subtype, balances, and transaction records.
  • On disconnection or offboarding, YCM calls Plaid /item/remove, marks the connection revoked, and deletes the encrypted token.

5. Risk Classification & Prohibited Uses

YCM’s entire customer base is classified as low-risk residential community management. The following are prohibited and screened against at onboarding: online gaming, gambling, cannabis, adult services, money transmission / MSB activity, consumer lending, cryptocurrency, and any other high-risk vertical.

6. Additional Payment-Side Verification

For associations that enable payment acceptance, merchant identity verification (KYC) is additionally performed by Stripe Connect’s standard onboarding (business verification and control-person checks), layered on top of YCM’s own diligence above.

8. Recordkeeping & Retention

  • Due-diligence evidence, authorization records, consent records, and audit logs are retained.
  • Financial records are retained for seven (7) years; personal data is anonymized on approved deletion requests consistent with that obligation.
