## Why community tone matters

Board members are volunteers. They're not buyers in a procurement sense — they're neighbors who got elected to a board because someone had to. Approach them like you would a fellow board member at the next door association, not like a vendor with a pitch deck.

**Tone calibration vs. PM track:**

| Element | PM track | Board track |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| Opener | Operational efficiency | Recognition of volunteer effort |
| Pricing language | Per-complex/portfolio | Per-association annual savings |
| CTA | "15-minute call" | "I'd love to learn how you're handling X" |
| Implicit positioning | Tool upgrade | Skill upgrade + legal coverage |
| Friction acknowledgment | Tool sprawl, vendor coordination | Solo workload, compliance worry, board burnout |
| Urgency | Contract renewal cycles | None — pace matches their calendar |

Board sales cycles are LONGER than PM cycles. Don't try to compress them. The board often needs to talk to the rest of the board before any movement happens. Plan for 60-90 days from first email to signed pilot.

## Sequence overview

5 touches over 14 days (slightly slower cadence than PM track). Drop to inactive after the final touch; re-engage in 90 days.

| Day | Action | Channel | Notes |
|-----|--------|---------|-------|
| 1 | Email #1 — recognition + soft intro | Email | Lead with appreciation for self-managing |
| 4 | LinkedIn (if board member is on LinkedIn — many aren't) | LinkedIn | Skip if no presence |
| 7 | Email #2 — specific scenario follow-up | Email | One concrete operational pain anchor |
| 11 | Email #3 — community-proof anchor | Email | Mention another local board, generic enough to anonymize |
| 14 | Email #4 — final + clear opt-out | Email | Permission to ignore; door open at 90d |

**Phone:** Optional and case-dependent. Most board members are professionally busy and treat unsolicited calls as intrusive. Include a phone number in the signature, but do not cold-call unless the board treasurer is also a real-estate or finance professional (in which case call cadence per PM track is fine).

**Sender:** William Ruiz, founder. Personal-account voice. **Important:** sign with name + product + URL only — no "Operations Team" or "Sales Team" copy. Boards reply to humans, not departments.

**Personalization tokens:**
- `[Name]` — first name only
- `[Complex Name]` — short complex name (not "The Lakes at Manchester Condominium Association, Inc.")
- `[Town/City]` — used in opener if natural
- `[Specific detail]` — public info (number of units, recent local news mention, board minutes posted online, etc.). Skip if no usable detail; do NOT fabricate.

## Board-specific A/B test ideas

- **Subject line:** community-toned vs. operational-toned (variants A vs. B/C)
- **Pricing reveal:** include in Email #1 vs. defer to Email #3 — boards may pre-qualify out on price; equally, transparency builds trust
- **CTA structure:** "15-minute call" vs. "send the one-pager" — boards often want a doc to discuss internally before scheduling a call
- **Email #3 social proof:** generic anonymized example vs. named-with-permission case study (when available)
- **"For the board" framing:** is the email addressed to one board member ("Hi Sarah") or to "the board collectively" ("Hi [Complex Name] Board") — singular probably wins reply rate; plural may help with internal forwarding

---

## Anti-patterns (specifically harmful for board outreach)

- **Don't sound like a property management company** — boards are skeptical of anything that smells like an outside company taking control of their association
- **Don't create urgency** — "limited-time offer" / "deadline" framing destroys trust with volunteer board members
- **Don't pitch "scale" or "growth"** — most boards run one association forever; growth language signals you don't understand their world
- **Don't use SaaS jargon** — "stack," "platform-as-a-service," "data lake," "ROI" all alienate
- **Don't multi-thread** — emailing the president AND treasurer AND secretary simultaneously is a red flag. Pick one (usually president or treasurer) and respect their internal communication
- **Don't reference data you couldn't reasonably know** — "I see you have 47 units" is fine if it's on the SoS website; "I see you collected $32,500 in dues last quarter" is creepy
