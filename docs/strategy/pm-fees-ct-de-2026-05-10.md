## Why this doc exists

Every sales conversation — board or PM — anchors on cost. The board is comparing YCM's $89–$399/mo to what they pay (or could pay) a property manager. The PM is comparing YCM's $30–$50/complex to their own staff + tools overhead. This doc captures the CT/DE-specific reference numbers so reps don't have to re-research them mid-call.

These are working anchors, not contractual quotes. PM fees vary by complex size, age, amenity load, and management scope (full-service vs. financial-only vs. compliance-only). Always verify the prospect's actual cost during discovery.

---

## Headline figures

- **Average professional condo PM fee in CT/DE: $10–$20 per unit per month** (full-service management, residential condo association)
- **Common minimum monthly fee:** $300–$500/mo regardless of unit count (small associations pay disproportionately more per unit)
- **Setup / onboarding fees:** $500–$2,500 one-time, often discounted or waived in competitive markets
- **Annual contract:** standard. Many include 30-day or 60-day termination clauses.
- **CIOA (Common Interest Ownership Act) compliance** is a CT-specific overhead burden — many smaller boards self-manage to avoid the cost, but lack the tooling to do it well. This is YCM's primary opening for the Self-Managed Boards track.

## CT-specific market notes

### CIOA compliance overhead

Connecticut's Common Interest Ownership Act (CIOA, [Conn. Gen. Stat. §47-200 et seq.](https://www.cga.ct.gov/current/pub/chap_828.htm)) creates a specific compliance burden for condo associations:

- **Mandatory annual disclosures** — financial statements, reserve studies, governing documents available to owners on request
- **Statutory meeting + notice requirements** — annual meetings with specific notice periods, voting rules, quorum thresholds
- **Resale certificate (CIOA §47-270)** — must be provided to buyers within statutory timeframes
- **Board fiduciary duties** — codified personal-liability exposure for self-dealing or negligence

A board self-managing without proper documentation infrastructure can incur real liability. YCM's compliance tooling (document retention, meeting notices, reserve fund tracking, owner communications log) directly addresses this — **this is the wedge** for self-managed boards considering whether the cost of YCM is worth the legal coverage it provides.

### Why CT/DE specifically (vs. other northeastern states)

- **CT** — high density of small-to-medium condos (post-1980s development); CIOA creates documentation overhead; many self-managed boards operating informally with real legal exposure
- **DE** — friendly regulatory environment for associations; smaller population means smaller PM market with less competition; PM fees often higher per unit due to lack of scale
- Both states have aging stock with active reserve study + assessment cycles — meaning operational complexity is rising regardless of management model

## How to use these numbers in a sales call

**Discovery question:** "What does your association pay your current property manager each month?"

If they answer with a number → calculate annual cost → compare to their YCM tier → **state the savings**:

> *"You're at $750/month, that's $9,000 a year. The YCM tier that fits your unit count is $149/month — $1,788 a year. You'd save about $7,200 every year, plus get a platform that's purpose-built for condo workflows."*

If they self-manage → reframe to **legal coverage value**:

> *"Most boards I've talked to in CT spend 5–10 hours a week on association admin without any compliance backstop. CIOA exposes the board personally to documentation gaps. YCM gives you the tooling to handle that for under $90 a month — which is less than one billable hour from a lawyer."*

If they're a PM → reframe to **operational efficiency**:

> *"You're paying for software AND staff to handle what that software can't. YCM at $30/complex is roughly the cost of a single hour of admin time per complex per month — and it eliminates 5–10 hours of that work via automation."*
