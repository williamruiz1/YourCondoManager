## Entity Structure

**Form:** Delaware C-Corporation  
**Name:** YourCondoManager, Inc.  
**Rationale:** Acquisition exit target requires C-Corp for stock sale optionality, acquirer familiarity, and QSBS eligibility.

### Why not LLC
- Acquirers have standardized C-Corp playbooks; LLC requires bespoke deal structuring
- Stock sales (single ~20% capital gains rate) only work cleanly in C-Corp form
- QSBS exclusion unavailable to LLC members

### Why not S-Corp
- Single non-US shareholder (e.g. PE fund with foreign LPs) immediately terminates S status
- Non-starter for any institutional acquisition

## QSBS Strategy (IRC Section 1202)

**Decision:** Structure the exit to maximize the federal QSBS tax exclusion.

### How it works
- Qualified Small Business Stock held for 5+ years in a C-Corp qualifies for exclusion of the **greater of $10M or 10x adjusted basis** of gain from federal capital gains tax
- Founder shares issued at $0.0001/share = basis of ~$850 on 8.5M shares
- 10x basis = $8,500 — so the $10M floor applies
- **Up to $10M of gain is federally tax-free at exit**

### The equity structure decision
William's intent: **limit the equity sold in the QSBS window to $10M**, keeping remaining equity to sell separately post-exclusion window or via a secondary transaction. This maximizes the tax-free exclusion and defers/manages tax on the remainder.

Example math at exit:
- Acquisition at $25M valuation, William owns 100%
- Sell $10M of shares under QSBS → $0 federal tax on that tranche
- Sell remaining $15M equity → ~$3M federal capital gains (20%)
- vs. no QSBS: ~$5M federal tax on full $25M gain
- **Net savings: ~$2M federal tax**

### QSBS clock
- Starts on the date founder shares are issued
- Must hold for **5 full years** — do not transfer or convert shares
- Clock resets if entity converts from LLC to C-Corp (another reason to start as C-Corp)
- **Issue founder shares on the same day as incorporation — 2026-05-11**

### Preservation rules
- Keep the corporation under $50M in gross assets at time of stock issuance (easily met)
- Do not let the corporation become a service company (SaaS qualifies as a non-excluded trade)
- Do not repurchase more than 5% of outstanding stock in the year before/after issuance
