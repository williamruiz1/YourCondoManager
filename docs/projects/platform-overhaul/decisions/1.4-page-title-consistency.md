## Context

YCM pages today set their visible page title through a mix of `<h1>` tags, a shared `WorkspacePageHeader` component (`title="..."` prop), raw marketing-style section headers, and — in `owner-portal.tsx` — persona-adapted greetings ("Welcome, {firstName}") that double as page titles. The browser-level `document.title` is set once in `client/index.html` to the marketing string "Your Condo Manager - Condo Property Management Platform" and, aside from analytics reads (`App.tsx:906`, `cookie-consent-banner.tsx:59`), is never updated per route. There is no shared title-setter hook.

This fragmentation produces three visible problems:

1. **Breadcrumb vs title drift.** 0.3 Q4 AC 18 binds the breadcrumb leaf to the page title. Without a canonical title catalog, breadcrumb labels (1.3) cannot be verified against the titles actually rendered.
2. **"Dashboard" leakage.** 0.1 forbids "Dashboard" from the `/app`, `/app/portfolio`, and `/app/operations/dashboard` page titles. `dashboard.tsx:630` and `operations-dashboard.tsx:98` both currently render "Dashboard" in `WorkspacePageHeader`'s `title` prop, and `portfolio.tsx:263` renders "Portfolio at a Glance" — none of the three matches its 0.1-locked name.
3. **Tab / SEO identity is stuck on the marketing string.** Every `/app/*` and `/portal/*` route shares the same `<title>` at the browser level, which undermines multi-tab workflows (a Manager with Billing, Work Orders, and Payments open in three tabs sees three identical tab labels).

1.4 defines a canonical title catalog per route, a `document.title` format, and the governance mechanism that keeps both in sync with breadcrumbs (1.3) and nav labels (1.1 / 1.2 / 1.5). 1.4 does **not** redesign pages — it audits and standardises title copy and the surface that renders it.

### Cross-cutting findings

- **No `document.title` writer exists anywhere in the app.** Every tab displays the `index.html:6` marketing string.
- **Three title surfaces coexist** (`WorkspacePageHeader`, raw `<h1>`, dynamic data-driven titles). No component convention is enforced.
- **Owner Portal uses raw `<h1>` exclusively** — it does not use `WorkspacePageHeader`. Q7 decides whether it should.
- **Detail views** (`election-ballot.tsx:307`, `election-detail.tsx:742`) render dynamic titles (`{election.title}`). The catalog needs a rule for `/app/section/:id` patterns.

## Conflict Analysis

1. **0.1 violations in production.** Three titles locked by 0.1 ("Home", "Portfolio Health", "Operations Overview") are currently rendered as "Dashboard", "Portfolio at a Glance", and "Operations Dashboard". 1.4 must include the copy fix, not assume 0.1 was already implemented.
2. **Breadcrumb–title coupling (0.3 Q4 AC 18).** 1.3 and 1.4 cannot ship independently. A canonical title catalog that 1.3 can source from is required; otherwise AC 18 drifts on every title edit.
3. **Surface fragmentation.** `WorkspacePageHeader` vs raw `<h1>` vs dynamic greeting. Without a single surface (or strict convention), titles can be changed in one place without the other updating.
4. **`document.title` is effectively unimplemented.** The tab label provides no wayfinding. Any resolution must decide whether to centralise title-setting (hook, router integration, or per-page side effect) and what format applies.
5. **Owner Portal parallel convention.** `/portal/*` lives in one mega-file with its own header idiom. Q7 decides whether it adopts the `/app/*` convention or keeps a parallel catalog.
6. **"Dashboard" prohibition scope.** 0.1 forbids the word on three specific pages. Q8 decides whether the prohibition extends globally (e.g., future "Analytics Dashboard" surfaces) or remains a per-page rule.

## Open Questions

> None of Q1–Q8 have a selected resolution. Answers from William + YCM CC are required before any catalog entry moves out of PENDING.

- **Q1.** What is the canonical `document.title` format? Candidates include `{Page} · {Brand}` (e.g., "Billing · YCM"), `{Association} – {Page} | YCM` (e.g., "Maple Ridge – Billing | YCM"), `{Page} | Your Condo Manager`, or "keep static, do not set per page". Where is it applied centrally — a title-setter hook, the router, a layout effect, or per-page?
- **Q2.** Must the visible H1 equal the breadcrumb leaf in every case (0.3 Q4 AC 18 read strictly), or may the crumb be a shorter alias of a more descriptive H1 (e.g., H1 "Inspection Records" / crumb "Inspections")? If aliasing is allowed, where is the alias map stored?
- **Q3.** For section hubs introduced by 1.2 (per-section landing pages), does the H1 use the bare section name ("Finance") or a hub designator ("Finance Overview" / "Finance Home")? Implications for the "Dashboard" prohibition (Q8) if "Overview" becomes a hub suffix.
- **Q4.** For association-scoped pages, is the association name part of the H1 (e.g., "Maple Ridge — Billing"), or is association context relegated to a subheader / eyebrow / breadcrumb as 0.3 Q4 AC 17 already provides?
- **Q5.** For persona-specific landings that share a route (`/app` → Manager full vs Board Admin scoped per 0.2), is the H1 the same word for both ("Home") or persona-adapted ("Portfolio Home" vs "Association Home")?
- **Q6.** Who governs the catalog — a central route→title mapping (one file, one source of truth), hardcoded per-page plus a linter/convention, or a hook-driven API (`useDocumentTitle`/`usePageTitle`)? This mirrors 1.3 Q5 on breadcrumb governance and likely shares the same answer.
- **Q7.** Does `/portal/*` follow the same title convention as `/app/*` (single `WorkspacePageHeader`-style surface, same `document.title` format), or maintain a parallel catalog optimised for the owner/mobile context?
- **Q8.** Is the "Dashboard" prohibition limited to the three pages named in 0.1 (`/app`, `/app/portfolio`, `/app/operations/dashboard`), or extended platform-wide to any future surface? What enforcement mechanism is acceptable (review checklist, ESLint rule against the string literal, test assertion, or a lint on the canonical catalog)?

## How This Informs Downstream Layers

### Layer 1 — IA Taxonomy Cleanup
- **1.1 (nav labels)** and **1.4 (page titles)** must converge where nav label == section hub title (per Q3 resolution). The catalog defined by 1.4 is the authoritative string for the sidebar item that enters a section hub.
- **1.2 (section hub pattern)** consumes Q3 to decide whether hub H1s carry an "Overview" / "Home" suffix.
- **1.3 (breadcrumbs)** consumes the 1.4 catalog directly: 0.3 Q4 AC 18 binds the leaf crumb to the page title. 1.3 cannot ship stable until 1.4's catalog is LOCKED.

### Layer 2 — Role & Permission Model
No direct dependency. 2.3 / 2.4 permission gating is orthogonal to page titles, except that persona-adapted titles (Q5) must be enforced at the same layer as the role gate.

### Layer 3 — Navigation Restructure
3.1–3.3 consume the title catalog as the source of truth for sidebar labels, header chrome, and tab `document.title`. The Q6 governance decision determines whether 3.x reads from a shared module or a hook.

### Layer 4 — Feature Gaps
4.1 (central updates/inbox) needs a canonical page title ("Inbox" vs "Updates" vs "Notifications") — Q1 + Q7 jointly decide.

### Layer 5 — Polish & Hardening
5.x mobile chrome and 5.x a11y audits depend on the title catalog being fixed. The `document.title` format (Q1) becomes part of the SEO/analytics hardening pass.
