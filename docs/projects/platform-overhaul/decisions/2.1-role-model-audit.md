## Context

0.2 locked the canonical persona model: **Manager**, **Board Officer**, **Assisted Board**, **PM Assistant**, **Owner**, **Platform Admin** — with explicit role-value bindings `manager`, `board-officer`, `assisted-board`, `pm-assistant`, `owner`, `platform-admin` (originally three personas with `board-admin`; expanded to six in 2026-04-16 rename). 0.3 Q5 locked **strict role isolation** as the platform-wide gating principle. 1.1 Q4 locked a per-page `persona:` tag comment that references the 0.2 boundary matrix.

The live codebase, however, speaks in a **four-role operator vocabulary** (`platform-admin | board-admin | manager | viewer`) enforced by both client-side sidebar gates and a server-side `requireAdminRole` middleware — plus a **four-role portal vocabulary** (`owner | tenant | readonly | board-member`) enforced by a separate `requirePortal` middleware. Neither vocabulary was authored by 0.2. The operator role `viewer` and the operator role `platform-admin` are not in the 0.2 persona map; neither is the portal role `tenant`, `readonly`, or `board-member`.

2.1 catalogues every role string in the codebase, flags the schema-vs-client mismatches, and enumerates the open questions YCM CC + founder must resolve before Layer 2 modules 2.2–2.4 (permission boundary corrections, role-gating audit, server-side enforcement) can be written. 2.1 does not decide whether `viewer` survives, whether `platform-admin` is a persona or an operational capability, whether the portal's four sub-roles collapse to one `owner` role per 0.2, or how schema-only role strings are reconciled with client-visible gating.

   > **0.2 §The Six Active Personas — LOCKED** enumerates six personas and binds role values: Manager → `manager`, Board Officer → `board-officer`, Assisted Board → `assisted-board`, PM Assistant → `pm-assistant`, Owner → `owner`, Platform Admin → `platform-admin`.

## Open Questions

- **Q1 — `platform-admin` reconciliation.** `platform-admin` is absent from 0.2 but present in schema, client, and server enforcement. Is it (a) a fourth operator persona to be added to 0.2 via amendment, (b) an internal YCM-operator capability distinct from the customer persona model and documented separately, or (c) a role that collapses into `manager` for persona purposes but retains a capability flag? Tradeoff axis: **persona purity (0.2 stays 3 personas) vs. operational reality (platform ops staff exist and gate on this role).**

- **Q2 — `viewer` reconciliation.** `viewer` is the default role on admin creation (`shared/schema.ts:165`) and is allow-listed on every read endpoint. Is it (a) a read-only variant of Manager/Board Officer/Assisted Board to be absorbed into a capability flag, (b) a persona add to 0.2, or (c) removed entirely and migrated to a scope attribute? Tradeoff axis: **personas vs. capabilities — should read-only be a role or a permission flag?**

- **Q3 — Portal sub-roles `tenant`, `readonly`, `board-member` reconciliation.** 0.2 lists only `owner` for `/portal`. Schema admits four values. Do these three extra portal roles (a) persist as sub-roles of the Owner persona with documented sub-persona scope, (b) collapse into `owner` with capability flags, or (c) get promoted to named personas in 0.2? Tradeoff axis: **persona simplicity vs. portal access-control granularity.**

- **Q4 — `AdminRole` type source of truth.** Three to four parallel declarations exist. Which module owns the canonical type export — `shared/schema.ts` (drizzle-zod-inferred), a new `shared/roles.ts`, or `client/src/lib/wip-features.ts`? Tradeoff axis: **cross-stack type reuse vs. client-bundle minimization.**

- **Q5 — `normalizeAdminRole` alias set.** `server/routes.ts:778-793` accepts 10 alias strings that are not in the schema enum. Are these (a) historical DB rows that still exist in production, requiring the normalizer to stay, (b) dead paths safe to remove, or (c) candidates for a one-time migration that then permits tightening the normalizer to the enum only? Tradeoff axis: **defensive coercion vs. schema-strict enforcement.**

- **Q6 — Default-role behavior.** `admin_users.role` defaults to `viewer`. If Q2 removes `viewer`, what is the new default — no default (require explicit role on creation), or `manager`? Tradeoff axis: **least-privilege default vs. operational onboarding friction.**

- **Q7 — `canAccess` null-role behavior.** `client/src/components/app-sidebar.tsx:211` returns `true` when role is nullish. Under 0.3 Q5 strict isolation, should this return `false`, or is the nullish-true branch covering a pre-auth render path that needs a different fix? Tradeoff axis: **strict isolation vs. pre-auth-render hydration shimmer.**

- **Q8 — `PortalRequest.portalRole` typing.** Typed as `string`. Should it be retyped to a union of `portalAccessRoleEnum` values, or does the bare `string` typing exist because `portalEffectiveRole` sometimes carries synthetic composite values (e.g., derived board-access strings)? Tradeoff axis: **type safety vs. runtime composability of effective-role strings.**

- **Q9 — Server-side enforcement gaps.** Of 544 route handlers, 108 do not carry `requireAdmin`. Public tokens, webhooks, and vendor portal account for most of the delta. Is any subset of these handlers (a) correctly public, (b) correctly scoped to `requirePortal` / `requireVendorPortal`, or (c) a gating bug? A handler-by-handler classification table is Q9's implementation output. Tradeoff axis: **strict enumeration (every route classified) vs. risk-weighted review (only routes touching mutable data).**

- **Q10 — Vendor portal persona in 0.2.** Vendor portal (`server/routes.ts:13355-13797+`) is a fifth auth domain not in 0.2. Is the Vendor persona (a) added to 0.2 via amendment, (b) documented in a new Layer 0 decision doc, or (c) confirmed out-of-scope for the overhaul and excluded from the Layer 2 gating catalog? Tradeoff axis: **persona-map completeness vs. overhaul-scope discipline.**

- **Q11 — Role-vs-visibility string namespace.** `hubVisibilityLevelEnum` shares string literals (`owner`, `board`, `admin`) with auth role enums. If role labels change (Q1/Q2/Q3 outcomes), do hub-visibility values move in lockstep, or is the hub-visibility vocabulary explicitly decoupled? Tradeoff axis: **single shared vocabulary vs. decoupled namespaces.**

## Selected Resolutions

### Q1 — `platform-admin` reconciliation

**Resolution:** `platform-admin` is a **fourth operator persona**, added to 0.2 via amendment. Definition: YCM staff or PM employee with controlled internal access. Not a customer persona. Scope: prevent association sabotage and human error by operating above the customer role tiers. Separate capability boundary from Manager — Manager owns a customer association's workspace; Platform Admin owns YCM-internal configuration, platform controls, ingestion, admin-user management, and executive reporting. The 0.2 amendment adding Persona 4 is queued as a separate commit (see Cross-Reference Notice at the top of this document).

### Q2 — `viewer` reconciliation

**Resolution:** Keep. `viewer` is a read-only operator role and remains in `adminUserRoleEnum` at `shared/schema.ts:161`. The least-privilege default on admin creation at `shared/schema.ts:165` stays `viewer`. Document `viewer` as a **capability variant of Manager** (read-only) rather than a fourth customer persona. It is not added to 0.2 as a persona; it is a capability flag expressed as a role value for schema simplicity.

### Q3 — Portal sub-roles `tenant`, `readonly`, `board-member` reconciliation

**Resolution:** Collapse all three into `owner`. 0.2 §Persona 3 names exactly one portal role (`owner`) and explicitly covers "A condo unit owner (or occupant with portal access)" — tenants are occupants under the single `owner` role. 2.2 Q1 (committed `ae76a3f`) already declared `portal_access.role = "board-member"` non-canonical with containment queued. By the same locked logic `tenant` and `readonly` are also non-canonical. Queue all three for containment/removal in a downstream migration.

### Q4 — `AdminRole` type source of truth

**Resolution:** Single canonical `AdminRole` type derived from `adminUserRoleEnum` in `shared/schema.ts:161` and exported from `shared/`. All client-side and server-side declarations (`client/src/App.tsx:89`, `client/src/components/app-sidebar.tsx:51`, `client/src/lib/wip-features.ts:1`, `server/routes.ts:754`) import from `shared/` rather than re-declaring. Dual `isSingleAssociationBoardExperience` helpers (`App.tsx:192`, `app-sidebar.tsx:216`) also collapse into a single shared export.

### Q5 — `normalizeAdminRole` alias set disposition

**Resolution:** Migrate and remove. 0.3 Q5 strict isolation forbids silent role coercion (the normalizer's fallback downgrade to `viewer` on unknown values is a security hazard). The Q5 implementation output is: (a) classify every `admin_users.role` row in production, (b) migrate alias rows to canonical enum values, (c) remove `normalizeAdminRole` (or constrain it to identity over the four-value enum). Migration audit and removal are build-time work scheduled when 2.1 enters build.

### Q6 — Default-role behavior

**Resolution:** Default stays `viewer` (inherits from Q2 founder decision). `shared/schema.ts:165` default value preserved. Least-privilege semantics on admin creation retained.

### Q7 — `canAccess` null-role behavior

**Resolution:** `canAccess()` at `client/src/components/app-sidebar.tsx:209-213` must return `false` when role is nullish. 0.3 AC 21 is binding: "Pages and navigation items are hidden when the role lacks access." The pre-auth render path (the reason for the current permissive default) must be solved with a separate session-loading guard — e.g., suppress sidebar render until session resolves — not by permissive role check.

### Q8 — `PortalRequest.portalRole` typing

**Resolution:** Retype to enum literal. With Q3 collapsing portal roles to `owner`, `PortalRequest.portalRole` (`server/routes.ts:767`) and `PortalRequest.portalEffectiveRole` (`:770`) become unions derived from `portalAccessRoleEnum`. Post-containment migration (Q3 output), this narrows to a single literal `"owner"`. No bare `string` typing at the TS boundary.

### Q9 — Server-side enforcement gap classification

**Resolution:** Produce a full 544-handler classification table as implementation output. 0.3 Q5 strict isolation requires every handler accounted for. Classification categories: `requireAdmin*` (operator-gated), `requirePortal*` (portal-gated), `requireVendorPortal` (vendor-gated), `public-by-design` (token-scoped: `/api/elections/ballot/:token`, `/api/portal/payments/link/:token`; webhooks; health endpoint), `gating-bug` (missing gate — remediation required). Table committed under 2.1's implementation output path when 2.1 enters build.

### Q10 — Vendor portal persona in 0.2

**Resolution:** Out of scope for this overhaul. Vendor portal (`server/routes.ts:13355-13797+`) is flagged for a future Layer 0 decision doc. No changes to vendor portal gating or role vocabulary in Layers 1–5 of this overhaul. `requireVendorPortal` and `VendorPortalRequest` remain unchanged; the vendor role domain is not added to 0.2.

### Q11 — Role-vs-visibility string namespace

**Resolution:** Decouple. `hubVisibilityLevelEnum` at `shared/schema.ts:2599` currently shares literals `owner`, `board`, `admin` with auth role strings — a latent rename hazard. Rename hub-visibility values to a distinct vocabulary (e.g., `public | residents | unit-owners | board-only | operator-only`) in a downstream migration. Policy locked here; migration scope and consumer enumeration are build-time work.
