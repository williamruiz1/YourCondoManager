## Context

0.2 locked the canonical three-persona model: **Manager**, **Board Admin**, **Owner** — with explicit role-value bindings `manager`, `board-admin`, `owner`. 0.3 Q5 locked **strict role isolation** as the platform-wide gating principle. 1.1 Q4 locked a per-page `persona:` tag comment that references the 0.2 boundary matrix.

The live codebase, however, speaks in a **four-role operator vocabulary** (`platform-admin | board-admin | manager | viewer`) enforced by both client-side sidebar gates and a server-side `requireAdminRole` middleware — plus a **four-role portal vocabulary** (`owner | tenant | readonly | board-member`) enforced by a separate `requirePortal` middleware. Neither vocabulary was authored by 0.2. The operator role `viewer` and the operator role `platform-admin` are not in the 0.2 persona map; neither is the portal role `tenant`, `readonly`, or `board-member`.

2.1 catalogues every role string in the codebase, flags the schema-vs-client mismatches, and enumerates the open questions YCM CC + founder must resolve before Layer 2 modules 2.2–2.4 (permission boundary corrections, role-gating audit, server-side enforcement) can be written. 2.1 does not decide whether `viewer` survives, whether `platform-admin` is a persona or an operational capability, whether the portal's four sub-roles collapse to one `owner` role per 0.2, or how schema-only role strings are reconciled with client-visible gating.

## Conflict Analysis

1. **Operator role vocabulary is four values; 0.2 persona map is two (Manager, Board Admin).** `platform-admin` and `viewer` exist in `adminUserRoleEnum` (`shared/schema.ts:161`) and are enforced throughout `server/routes.ts` and `client/src/App.tsx` but appear in **no cell** of the 0.2 persona matrix. Maps to **Q1** and **Q2**.

   > ⚠️ CROSS-REFERENCE NOTICE
   > **0.2 §The Three Active Personas — LOCKED** enumerates exactly three personas and binds role values: Manager → `manager`, Board Admin → `board-admin`, Owner → `owner`.
   > **Conflicting evidence:** `shared/schema.ts:161` defines four operator roles including `platform-admin` and `viewer`; `client/src/App.tsx:123-181` and `server/routes.ts:1025-1049` both gate live surfaces on those two strings.
   > **0.2 AC reference:** 0.2 Acceptance Criteria do not explicitly forbid `platform-admin` or `viewer`, but the persona-map section and matrix are silent on them — they are neither surfaced nor gated by 0.2.
   > Flag only. Do not resolve.

2. **Portal role vocabulary is four values; 0.2 persona map is one (Owner).** `portalAccessRoleEnum` at `shared/schema.ts:1084` admits `owner | tenant | readonly | board-member`. 0.2 lists only `owner` as the portal role. `tenant`, `readonly`, and `board-member` portal role strings are used by `resolvePortalAccessContext` (consumed at `server/routes.ts:1083-1092`) and by `requirePortalBoard` (via `portalHasBoardAccess`). Maps to **Q3**.

3. **Three parallel `AdminRole` type declarations.** `client/src/App.tsx:89`, `client/src/components/app-sidebar.tsx:51`, and `client/src/lib/wip-features.ts:1` all re-declare the same type; `server/routes.ts:754` declares a fourth copy. No single source of truth import. Maps to **Q4**.

4. **Server-side `normalizeAdminRole` silently coerces 10 alias strings.** `server/routes.ts:778-793` accepts `admin`, `super-admin`, `superadmin`, `platform_admin`, `association-admin`, `board_admin`, `board-member`, `boardmember`, `association-manager`, `operator`, and silently downgrades any unknown value to `viewer`. None of these alias strings appear in `shared/schema.ts`. The schema enum would reject them on insert; the normalizer runs against already-persisted `admin_users.role` values. Maps to **Q5**.

12. **[NEW]** The 2.2 Q1 governance model introduces a structured mechanism — PM-configurable per-feature View/Write toggles — that may resolve several legacy board-admin role variants currently inflating the role count. Role strings like `board-member`, `board_admin`, `boardmember`, or similar variants that appear to approximate governance scoping should be evaluated against the PM-Managed Default Access Table (in 2.2 Q1) before any consolidation decision. Some may be legacy attempts to encode access policy in role strings rather than a toggle model. This does not resolve any 2.1 Q — it is context for the Q1–Q11 resolution pass. See 0.2 amendment at commit `2070b7e` and 2.2 Q1 Selected Resolution.

5. **`viewer` default on admin creation.** `shared/schema.ts:165` defaults `admin_users.role` to `"viewer"`. Every new admin created without an explicit role becomes a `viewer`. 0.2 does not define a viewer persona; `App.tsx:89` and every `requireAdminRole([..., "viewer"])` call include `viewer` on read endpoints. Maps to **Q1** and **Q6**.

6. **`canAccess` in app-sidebar returns `true` on null role.** `client/src/components/app-sidebar.tsx:211` returns `true` when role is nullish — the unauthenticated-by-sidebar path. This conflicts with 0.3 Q5 **strict role isolation** (AC 21: "Pages and navigation items are hidden when the role lacks access"). Maps to **Q7**.

   > ⚠️ CROSS-REFERENCE NOTICE
   > **0.3 AC 21 (LOCKED):** "Pages and navigation items are hidden when the role lacks access."
   > **Conflicting evidence:** `client/src/components/app-sidebar.tsx:209-213` — `canAccess()` returns `true` when `role` is `undefined` or `null`, i.e., unauthenticated callers see the full nav tree.
   > Flag only. Do not resolve.

7. **`PortalRequest.portalRole` is typed as bare `string`.** `server/routes.ts:767` — no enum constraint at the TS boundary. Downstream handlers that branch on portal role (search `req.portalRole` and `req.portalEffectiveRole` usages) therefore cannot be type-checked against `portalAccessRoleEnum`. Maps to **Q8**.

8. **`requireAdmin`-absent routes that touch admin-scoped data.** Of 544 handlers, 436 use `requireAdmin`; the delta includes token-scoped public routes (`/api/elections/ballot/:token` at `server/routes.ts:6590, 6619`, `/api/portal/payments/link/:token` at `server/routes.ts:4568, 4709`) that intentionally bypass admin auth, plus the vendor-portal suite. No evidence (in the files sampled) that any `/api/` handler is missing gating it should have — but a full handler-by-handler audit is Q9's scope. Maps to **Q9**.

9. **Vendor portal is a fifth role domain with no 0.2 entry.** `server/routes.ts:13355-13361` introduces `VendorPortalRequest` and `requireVendorPortal`. Neither 0.2 nor 0.3 references a Vendor persona. Maps to **Q10**.

10. **Dual redirect-helper declarations.** `isSingleAssociationBoardExperience` exists at `client/src/App.tsx:192` and at `client/src/components/app-sidebar.tsx:216` — two authoritative copies of a gating predicate. Maps to **Q4**.

11. **`hubVisibilityLevelEnum` overlaps role vocabulary.** `shared/schema.ts:2599` defines `public | resident | owner | board | admin` — a content-visibility tier that shares string values (`owner`, `board`, `admin`) with auth roles. Not an auth enum, but the shared string namespace is a latent rename hazard. Maps to **Q11**.

## Open Questions

- **Q1 — `platform-admin` reconciliation.** `platform-admin` is absent from 0.2 but present in schema, client, and server enforcement. Is it (a) a fourth operator persona to be added to 0.2 via amendment, (b) an internal YCM-operator capability distinct from the customer persona model and documented separately, or (c) a role that collapses into `manager` for persona purposes but retains a capability flag? Tradeoff axis: **persona purity (0.2 stays 3 personas) vs. operational reality (platform ops staff exist and gate on this role).**

- **Q2 — `viewer` reconciliation.** `viewer` is the default role on admin creation (`shared/schema.ts:165`) and is allow-listed on every read endpoint. Is it (a) a read-only variant of Manager/Board Admin to be absorbed into a capability flag, (b) a persona add to 0.2, or (c) removed entirely and migrated to a scope attribute? Tradeoff axis: **personas vs. capabilities — should read-only be a role or a permission flag?**

- **Q3 — Portal sub-roles `tenant`, `readonly`, `board-member` reconciliation.** 0.2 lists only `owner` for `/portal`. Schema admits four values. Do these three extra portal roles (a) persist as sub-roles of the Owner persona with documented sub-persona scope, (b) collapse into `owner` with capability flags, or (c) get promoted to named personas in 0.2? Tradeoff axis: **persona simplicity vs. portal access-control granularity.**

- **Q4 — `AdminRole` type source of truth.** Three to four parallel declarations exist. Which module owns the canonical type export — `shared/schema.ts` (drizzle-zod-inferred), a new `shared/roles.ts`, or `client/src/lib/wip-features.ts`? Tradeoff axis: **cross-stack type reuse vs. client-bundle minimization.**

- **Q5 — `normalizeAdminRole` alias set.** `server/routes.ts:778-793` accepts 10 alias strings that are not in the schema enum. Are these (a) historical DB rows that still exist in production, requiring the normalizer to stay, (b) dead paths safe to remove, or (c) candidates for a one-time migration that then permits tightening the normalizer to the enum only? Tradeoff axis: **defensive coercion vs. schema-strict enforcement.**

- **Q6 — Default-role behavior.** `admin_users.role` defaults to `viewer`. If Q2 removes `viewer`, what is the new default — no default (require explicit role on creation), or `manager`? Tradeoff axis: **least-privilege default vs. operational onboarding friction.**

- **Q7 — `canAccess` null-role behavior.** `client/src/components/app-sidebar.tsx:211` returns `true` when role is nullish. Under 0.3 Q5 strict isolation, should this return `false`, or is the nullish-true branch covering a pre-auth render path that needs a different fix? Tradeoff axis: **strict isolation vs. pre-auth-render hydration shimmer.**

- **Q8 — `PortalRequest.portalRole` typing.** Typed as `string`. Should it be retyped to a union of `portalAccessRoleEnum` values, or does the bare `string` typing exist because `portalEffectiveRole` sometimes carries synthetic composite values (e.g., derived board-access strings)? Tradeoff axis: **type safety vs. runtime composability of effective-role strings.**

- **Q9 — Server-side enforcement gaps.** Of 544 route handlers, 108 do not carry `requireAdmin`. Public tokens, webhooks, and vendor portal account for most of the delta. Is any subset of these handlers (a) correctly public, (b) correctly scoped to `requirePortal` / `requireVendorPortal`, or (c) a gating bug? A handler-by-handler classification table is Q9's implementation output. Tradeoff axis: **strict enumeration (every route classified) vs. risk-weighted review (only routes touching mutable data).**

- **Q10 — Vendor portal persona in 0.2.** Vendor portal (`server/routes.ts:13355-13797+`) is a fifth auth domain not in 0.2. Is the Vendor persona (a) added to 0.2 via amendment, (b) documented in a new Layer 0 decision doc, or (c) confirmed out-of-scope for the overhaul and excluded from the Layer 2 gating catalog? Tradeoff axis: **persona-map completeness vs. overhaul-scope discipline.**

- **Q11 — Role-vs-visibility string namespace.** `hubVisibilityLevelEnum` shares string literals (`owner`, `board`, `admin`) with auth role enums. If role labels change (Q1/Q2/Q3 outcomes), do hub-visibility values move in lockstep, or is the hub-visibility vocabulary explicitly decoupled? Tradeoff axis: **single shared vocabulary vs. decoupled namespaces.**
