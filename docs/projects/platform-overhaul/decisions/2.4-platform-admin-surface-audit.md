## Context

0.2 (LOCKED) defines three customer personas — Manager, Board Admin, Owner — and an explicit surface-ownership matrix. The `platform-admin` role is NOT in that matrix: 0.2 treats platform-admin as an operator persona that sits outside the three customer personas, and the 0.2 matrix includes no cells for platform-only surfaces (`/app/platform/*`, `/app/admin/*`, `/app/ai/*`). 0.3 Q5 (LOCKED) locks strict role isolation: pages and nav items must be hidden when the role lacks access, and read-only cross-role exposure requires explicit approval in spec. 1.1 (LOCKED) defines five functional zones for `/app` (Home, Financials, Operations, Governance, Communications) and four for `/portal` — none of which include a "Platform" zone for operator tooling.

The YCM codebase exposes a distinct surface area for YCM-internal platform operators: the `app-sidebar.tsx` "Platform" group (`client/src/components/app-sidebar.tsx:176-190`), a `platformSubPages` tab bar (`client/src/lib/sub-page-nav.ts:20`; mounted by `platform-controls.tsx:705`, `roadmap.tsx:641`, `executive.tsx:230`, `admin-users.tsx:116`), and multiple `/app/platform/*`, `/app/admin/*`, `/app/ai/*` route branches in `client/src/App.tsx:259-298`. The database enum `adminUserRoleEnum` at `shared/schema.ts:161` enumerates `["platform-admin", "board-admin", "manager", "viewer"]` — platform-admin is a first-class role, but no locked Layer 0 decision defines its surface boundary the way 0.2 defines the customer personas' boundary.

Phase 2 (`docs/specs/ia-audit-phase2-orphans.md`) issued DEMOTE-ADMIN verdicts on three platform pages (`platform-controls`, `ai-ingestion`, `roadmap`) and ORPHAN-SURFACE verdicts on two more (`admin-users`, `executive`). Phase 4 (`docs/specs/ia-audit-phase4-platform.md`) then catalogued concrete gating defects: three of the five platform routes have no inline role check in `App.tsx` (lines 259-262), the AI Ingestion UI is gated only by a WIP flag (`canAccessWipRoute`, `App.tsx:293`) rather than a role check, a large share of `/api/platform/*` read endpoints expose platform configuration data (tenant config, permission envelopes, email logs, provider status) to board-admin and manager, and all `/api/admin/roadmap`, `/api/admin/projects`, `/api/admin/workstreams`, `/api/admin/tasks` CRUD routes are open to board-admin and manager. The `platformSubPages` tab bar (`client/src/App.tsx:177-180`) also lists Admin Roadmap and Executive with `roles: ["platform-admin", "board-admin"]`, widening nav exposure beyond the Platform sidebar group's `["platform-admin"]` gate.

2.4 audits every platform/admin surface against the locked 0.2 matrix, the 0.3 Q5 strict-isolation principle, and the five-zone vocabulary of 1.1 (which has no "Platform" zone), surfacing the evidence and the open questions YCM CC + founder must resolve before Layer 3 can build a role-filtered sidebar that excludes platform operator tooling from customer personas without regression.

**2.2 Q1 governance model (added 2026-04-15).** The 2.2 Q1 board-admin governance model clarifies one boundary relevant to 2.4: Board Admin in PM-managed associations has no access to platform or admin surfaces by default (see PM-Managed Default Access Table in 2.2 Q1, row "Platform / admin surfaces"). The PM toggle model does not include platform or admin surfaces as configurable — those remain operator-level only. This eliminates one potential edge-case vector (a PM accidentally toggling board-admin into a platform surface) and should be reflected in the 2.4 enforcement analysis. Mode determination uses `tenant_configs.managementType` (`shared/schema.ts:1189`); see 0.2 amendment at commit `2070b7e` and 2.2 Q1 Selected Resolution.

### Cross-cutting observations

1. **No Layer 0 lock defines the platform-admin surface boundary.** 0.2 enumerates three personas (Manager, Board Admin, Owner) with a boundary matrix — platform-admin is not represented (`0.2-persona-map.md:110-129`). 1.1's five-zone taxonomy (Home, Financials, Operations, Governance, Communications) has no slot for platform operator tooling (`1.1-zone-taxonomy-corrections.md:39-47`). All platform surfaces therefore operate outside the locked taxonomy.
2. **Sidebar gate vs tab-bar gate disagree.** `app-sidebar.tsx:184-187` lists Platform-group children as `roles: ["platform-admin"]`. `client/src/App.tsx:177-178` lists the same pages in `platformSubPages` with `roles: ["platform-admin", "board-admin"]`. A board-admin viewing a Platform page via direct URL sees the tab bar and can navigate laterally into Admin Roadmap and Executive.
3. **Owner Portal link inside platform-admin nav.** `app-sidebar.tsx:185` nests `{ title: "Owner Portal", url: "/portal", roles: ["platform-admin"] }` inside the platform-admin-only group. `platformSubPages` (`App.tsx:180`) repeats this. 0.2 places `/portal` strictly under the Owner persona. Phase 4 §6 flagged this as a placement defect.
4. **Three of five `/app/admin/*` or `/app/ai/*` routes have no inline role check** (`App.tsx:259,260,261,262,292-294`). Only `/app/platform/controls` enforces `adminRole === "platform-admin"` at the route level (`App.tsx:296-298`).
5. **The `/app/admin` alias resolves to `RoadmapPage`** without any nav entry pointing to it (`App.tsx:259`) — a hidden alias to a platform-admin surface that a non-platform-admin can reach by URL.
6. **No 1.1 zone tag or persona tag has been applied to any of the five platform page files.** The zone-tag comment block mandated by 1.1 Q4 cannot be applied because no platform zone is defined in 1.1.

## Conflict Analysis

The audit surfaces seven decision axes. Each maps to an open question below.

1. **Platform-admin is absent from 0.2's boundary matrix.** 0.2 (LOCKED) defines surface ownership for Manager, Board Admin, and Owner only. Every `/app/platform/*`, `/app/admin/*`, and `/app/ai/ingestion` surface is implicitly "platform-admin-only" in operator intent, yet has no row in 0.2's matrix. Maps to **Q1**.

2. **Strict role isolation (0.3 Q5) is not enforced at the route layer.** `App.tsx:259-262` has zero inline role gating on four platform routes. `App.tsx:292-294` uses a WIP flag, not a role check. 0.3 AC 21 requires that pages and nav items be hidden when the role lacks access. Maps to **Q2**.

3. **API role width contradicts the implicit platform-admin boundary.** All `/api/ai/ingestion/*` writes allow board-admin and manager (`routes.ts:7484,7518,7550,7638,7669,7754,7774,7824,8097`). All `/api/admin/roadmap`, `/api/admin/projects`, `/api/admin/workstreams`, `/api/admin/tasks` CRUD allows board-admin and manager (Phase 4 §5 Z3-2). Multiple `/api/platform/*` reads are four-role-wide (`routes.ts:9676,9730,9767,10030,10039,10047,10077,10161,10169,10208,10219`). Maps to **Q3**.

4. **Tab-bar exposure widens client surface beyond sidebar gate.** `App.tsx:177-180` `platformSubPages` lists Admin Roadmap and Executive as `roles: ["platform-admin", "board-admin"]` — contradicting `app-sidebar.tsx:186-187` which gates them to platform-admin only. Maps to **Q4**.

5. **Owner Portal link sits inside platform-admin nav.** `app-sidebar.tsx:185` and `App.tsx:180` both put `/portal` inside a platform-admin-only group. 0.2 places `/portal` under the Owner persona. Maps to **Q5**.

6. **No 1.1 zone accommodates platform-admin surfaces.** 1.1 Q1 locks exactly five `/app` zones: Home, Financials, Operations, Governance, Communications. Platform pages fit none. 1.1 Q4 requires a zone tag on every page file — impossible for platform pages under the current vocabulary. Maps to **Q6**.

7. **Diagnostic surfaces (provider status, runtime status, monitoring, email logs, audit trails) have no policy.** Phase 4 §5 catalogued these as "read gaps" but did not distinguish diagnostic observability from configuration surfaces. 0.3 Q5 requires explicit spec approval for read-only cross-role exposure — none exists. Maps to **Q7**.

## Open Questions

### Q1 — Platform-admin boundary amendment to 0.2

Should 0.2's persona matrix be amended to include a fourth persona (platform-admin, YCM internal operator) with an explicit surface ownership row covering `/app/platform/*`, `/app/admin/*`, `/app/ai/ingestion`, or should 2.4 define the platform-admin boundary as a standalone Layer 2 artifact that is referenced from 0.2 but does not modify 0.2's three-customer-persona matrix?
**Tradeoff axis:** amend locked Layer 0 to make platform-admin a first-class persona (higher coherence, one source of truth) vs keep 0.2's customer focus and define platform-admin in Layer 2 (lower blast radius on locked docs, but fragments persona definitions).

### Q2 — Client route-guard policy for platform surfaces

What is the policy for client-side route guards on every `/app/platform/*`, `/app/admin/*`, and `/app/ai/*` route — inline `adminRole === "platform-admin"` per-route check matching `App.tsx:296-298`, a centralized `PlatformAdminRoute` wrapper, or a route-tree split (`/app/operator/*`) with a single entrypoint guard?
**Tradeoff axis:** per-route inline (lowest change, matches existing pattern) vs wrapper component (DRY, one change point) vs tree split (architecturally clean, largest refactor).

### Q3 — Server role-width policy for platform APIs

What is the server-side role-width policy for `/api/platform/*`, `/api/admin/*`, and `/api/ai/ingestion/*` — narrow every platform route to `["platform-admin"]`, retain read exposure for operators (board-admin, manager) while narrowing writes, or maintain current width pending a dedicated server-gating module?
**Tradeoff axis:** strict narrow (cleanest, highest break risk — some customer flows may rely on the widened reads) vs read-wide/write-narrow (conservative, preserves any intentional transparency use) vs defer (status quo, keeps C3 gap open).

### Q4 — Tab-bar vs sidebar gating reconciliation

Should `client/src/App.tsx:177-180` `platformSubPages` role lists (`["platform-admin", "board-admin"]` on Admin Roadmap and Executive) be narrowed to match the sidebar group's `["platform-admin"]`, or is board-admin tab-bar access intentional for a future read-only transparency feature and therefore to be preserved with an explicit AC?
**Tradeoff axis:** unify to platform-admin only (strict isolation per 0.3 Q5) vs preserve board-admin read (requires explicit spec approval per 0.3 Q5 AC 22 and a corresponding 0.2 matrix amendment).

### Q5 — Owner Portal launcher inside platform-admin nav

Should the `/portal` ("Owner Portal") entries in `app-sidebar.tsx:185` and `App.tsx:180` be removed, moved to a clearly labeled "External / Customer-facing surfaces" section of the platform-admin nav, or kept as-is?
**Tradeoff axis:** remove (cleanest persona isolation per 0.2) vs relabel/relocate (preserves operator-useful shortcut with honest labeling) vs keep (status quo, violates 0.2 persona boundary implicit policy).

### Q6 — Platform zone in 1.1 taxonomy

Should 1.1 be amended to add a sixth zone ("Platform" or "Operator") for platform-admin surfaces, or should platform surfaces be explicitly excluded from 1.1's zone-tag requirement with a documented exception, or should platform surfaces be moved to a separate route tree (e.g., `/operator/*`) outside `/app/*` and therefore outside 1.1's scope?
**Tradeoff axis:** amend 1.1 (one vocabulary for all `/app/*`, requires Layer 0/1 re-lock) vs exception carve-out (preserves 1.1 lock, fragments tag coverage) vs route-tree split (cleanest architectural boundary, largest implementation cost, coupled to Q2).

### Q7 — Diagnostic surface policy

What is the policy for platform diagnostic / observability surfaces (provider status, runtime status, monitoring, email logs, email tracking, audit trails, payment events, webhook secrets read, permission envelopes read, tenant config read) — treat as platform-admin-only like configuration, define a distinct "Diagnostic" read tier available to operator roles, or leave individually classified per endpoint?
**Tradeoff axis:** strict (one rule, maximum isolation, may over-restrict legitimate operator debugging) vs tiered (nuanced, requires a new role concept or envelope scope) vs per-endpoint (status quo, perpetuates the C3 gap catalog).
